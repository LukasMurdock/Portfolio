ABOUT ME


  • Hello! My name is Jay Shin. I am currently a Land Grant Scholar at The Ohio State University and just finished my 2nd year. I am studying Computer Science and Engineering towards a Bachelor's Degree in Spring 2024.

    At Ohio State, I work as an Undergraudate Teaching Assistant for an Intro Python Programming course. During the summer, I will be working at Sunquest Information Systems as a Software Development Engineer (SDE) Intern. I am very excited for this opportunity.

RUNNING

  • I started running in 8th grade to make friends, and even though I was not fast by any means, I was constatnly beating my previous personal bests in every meet. The fact that I was making myself a little faster after EVERY run fascinated me, and made me want to train harder and test my limit. Then, I met Coach Walters.

    I ran XC and Track and Field for Orange High School (2016-2020), and was coached by Adam Walters, a former runner at Otterbein University. I increased my mileage and started having a much more organized training. I worked my butt off and by the time I was an upperclassman, I was running with the top dogs on the varsity team before I knew it. In my senior year, I was running 60-65 miles a week and I honestly cared more about running than school. In a white-dominated sport, I wanted to prove that a nerdy Asian can invalidate the disadvantaged gene by outworking everyone.

    Here are some of the notable achevements I made throughout my running career. I made Academic All-State Honors twice (XC and T&F in 2019) and All-Conference Honorable Mention in XC (2019), both in Division 1. I hold a Freshman 4x800m school record for Orange High School since 2017. Lastly, I am a 8 x recipient of the Scholar Athlete Award from Orange.

    I ran for 1.5 years with the Ohio State Running Club, but after a careful consideration, I decided to completely retire from running in December 2021. My lifetime bests are 4:38 in the mile and 16:32 in the 5K, at the age of 19.
