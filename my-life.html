RUNNING

  • While transitioning into living in America, I started running in 8th grade to make friends. We had practices everyday and meets once every week. I was not fast by any means, but I was constatnly beating my previous personal bests in every meet. The fact that I was making myself slighlty faster after EVERY run fascinated me, and made me want to train harder and test my limit. Then, I met Coach Walters.

    I ran XC and Track and Field for Olentangy Orange High School (2016-2020), and was coached by Adam Walters, a former runner at Otterbein University. I increased my mileage and started having a much more organized training. I worked my butt off consistently and by the time I was an upperclassman, I was running with the top dogs before I knew it. In my senior year, I was running 60-65 miles a week and I honestly cared more about running than school. In a white-dominated sport, I wanted to prove that a nerdy Asian can invalidate the disadvantaged gene by outworking everyone.

    Here are some of the notable acheivements I've made in my running career. I received Academic All-State Honors twice in 2019, as well as an Honorable Mention in a Conference meet. I hold a school record in Freshman 4x800m for my high school since 2017. Too bad I didn't have a senior season.

    I ran with the Ohio State Running Club for 1.5 years, but after a careful consideration, I decided to retire from running in December 2021. My lifetime bests are 4:38 in the mile and 16:32 in the 5K, at the age of 19.

MUSIC

  • When I am not in school, I spend most of my time playing music. My love for music started in 2013 with a $50 acousic guitar. I am interested in diverse genres of music and I have particiapted in many different activities and groups in my life.

    Guitar is my main musical instrument and I play fingerstyle guitar, specifically. I am an experienced live performer as well. I have played at many open mics and weddings during high school. I volunteered at VITAS Healthcare for a year under the supervision of Dolores Hickman. I was assigned to perform at Paramount Senior Living at Westerville every weekend.

    I played the snare drum in my high schoool's marching band, under the direction of Craig Dunn from RhythmX. After practicing 24/7, I made the snareline in my first year of drumming. We marched 40+ halftime shows, parades, competitions, and concerts in one semester, while practicing everyday. It was a valuable experience that broadened my musical knowledge, but running was more important to me at the time lol. I only played for one year.

    I played the violin in high school's orchestra under the direction of Lori Cornett. We had 4 concerts every year and participated in the OMEA's Solo-and Ensemble Competitions, both as a group and individuals. I honestly didn't try very hard in orchestra, but I had a lot of fun. Oh, I was also selected to perform Handel's Messiah with the Columbus Symphony Orchestra.
